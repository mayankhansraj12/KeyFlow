# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in KeyFlow a welcoming, respectful,
and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background,
identity, experience level, or ability.

## Expected behavior

- Be considerate, constructive, and specific.
- Critique ideas and code without attacking people.
- Respect different viewpoints and levels of experience.
- Give and accept feedback in good faith.
- Protect the privacy and security of other contributors and users.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or unwanted attention.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Deliberate disruption, trolling, or sustained bad-faith participation.
- Sharing private user input, screenshots, credentials, or vulnerability
  details in public project spaces.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions, comments, issues, or other
participation that violates these expectations. They may temporarily or
permanently restrict participation when necessary to protect the community.

KeyFlow does not yet operate a dedicated conduct mailbox. To submit a private
conduct report, use
[GitHub private reporting](https://github.com/mayankhansraj12/KeyFlow/security/advisories/new)
and begin the title with `Code of Conduct report`. Do not include conduct
reports in public issues. Security vulnerabilities should follow
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

If a report concerns the repository owner, use
[GitHub's abuse-reporting channel](https://github.com/contact/report-abuse)
instead of the project-maintainer channel.

Maintainers will handle reports as confidentially as practical and avoid
conflicts of interest when reviewing them.
